Gameplay

Having graduated from college, Cass was in desperate need of a job. He lived with his parents, who constantly reminded him that his life was going nowhere -- a fact that he was fully aware of. Eventually he got desperate and responded to a seedy job offer, unwittingly selling his soul and becoming a maid for witches, ghouls, ghosts, and everthing you’ve ever feared.

Whether he’s good or not at his job is up to you.

How to Win

All you need to do is: stay alive and there's only one way to keep Cass alive: He just needs to do his job

Really well.

Keep the guests of the mansion satisfied with spiritual offerings and accommodations. If you neglect just one guest, they will become agitated and wreak havoc. If Cass’s health reaches 0 before the end of the night...well let's just say he got fired.

Health Bar and User Interface

Séance features a Doom-esque health “bar” in its UI. The goal was to have something a little more fun than a standard health bar by paying homage to an earlier classic in Doom’s health bar. Let’s call it a health face. Cass’s face represents his state: the less health he has, the more ragged his health face becomes, eventually becoming withered and pale in complexion. This gave users a visual cue that could give as much information as a normal health bar, in addition for it being fun for us to implement. In sum, this really puts the “face” in “interface”. The health face provides a good base for other UI elements to be placed around it. The player’s resources, cycle count, and time until the next cycle can all be seen at a glance in the upper-left corner. The player also has the ability to zoom in and out, allowing for a player to choose between zooming in to see the details of the map or zooming out to get a bigger, strategic picture.

Fog of War

Every 4 cycles, an enveloping Darkness surrounds the player, impacting their visibility throughout the mansion. In order to widen their visibility, the player must use the Candles at their disposal. After 4 cycles the Darkness will fade.

Neighbor Dynamics

Much of the strategic layer of Séance lies in the implications of residents and their neighbors. Because certain residents have a certain affinity or aversion to other residents, the player must learn about their guests to truly master the game. Moving residents to different rooms, getting rid of the high-threat monsters, and general map awareness can go a long way in the haunted mansion.

Agitation Dynamics

Monster all have a global agitation gain of 1 every 5 seconds. When monsters reach max agitation as specified by their unique entity attributes, they will go on a rampage and cause neighboring monsters, within the agitated monster’s area of effect to rapidly gain agitation. Furthermore, this area of effect will also affect the Player’s health if the Player stands for too long inside the area. Of the Player’s total health pool of 100, each second reduces the Player’s health by 25.

Characters

Throughout the game characters from all nightmares and worlds will appear demanding your service. The sound of a knock will symbolize the arrival of a new randomized character in the map. With every character, comes new unique attributes. Look through each individual character currently implemented and new characters that will be coming soon!

Witch

A strange shut-in.

She doesn’t like people coming into her room unannounced. Although she keeps to herself and typically won’t bother others, much of her time is spent thinking of nasty spells and hexes that she won’t hesitate to cast if disturbed.

Because of her great arcane prowess, nearby neighbors can be affected by her spells as well, agitating them in turn.

Due to her mature nature, she doesn’t get along with the ghost because she views their kind as very childish. With that said, she has a lot more patience than Frankenstein because she is used to dealing with such behavior.

Frankenstein

A docile giant who doesn’t mind sound and is a flexible neighbor.

No, really, he’s actually quite limber, but he’s also flexible in the sense that he gets along with most others. However, he has a larger than average diet and will go on a rampage, breaking down neighbors’ doors if left unattended to the point of agitation.

Frankenstein’s ease of placement, for example, he gets along with all neighbors, provides the player with a sort of “free” guest. The player does not have to worry about him getting along with others. He does, however, require the player to keep track of their resources to make sure they have enough food for him before he gets angry and starts becoming a problem.

Ghost

No one ghost is alike, just like people -- they were people at one point after all. Some have annoyingly particular accommodations and some are fine with anything. Their behavior upon agitation will vary.

Since every ghost is different, their game behavior will vary as well. Some of this behaviors are their speed, and maximum agitation. In addition, they are one of the faster monsters in the game. A similarity between the ghosts and the witches is that they both are not quite fond with each other, i.e. their agitation increase rate would be greater when they are neighbors. Unlike the witch, however, their maximum agitation is less than the witch to represent their childish and impatient nature.

Dracula

A surprisingly easy-going guy for someone so bloodthirsty. He’s a very particular eater, but generally easy to accommodate. However, he’ll attack his neighbors (and you) for blood upon agitation, which is usually caused by not feeding him. Dracula will also get a bit stronger every time he kills something, so watch out!

Dracula will get stronger if he kills another resident. This design necessitates that players keep track of him before he becomes overpoweringly strong. While having Dracula as a guest can be harrowing, it is balanced out by the fact that he is, for the most part, easy to accommodate. As long as the player can remain cognizant of his appetite, the player can certainly tend to other guests while appeasing Dracula. One may even want to let Dracula to kill another guest rather than using a silver bullet, keeping in mind that he could just as easily kill the player if he or she is in the wrong place at the wrong time.

Bloody Mary

A polite and considerate guest. Doesn’t eat much and doesn’t make too much of a fuss. Her bleeding head tends to make a mess periodically, though. Upon agitation she will go insane and start haunting her neighbors, making them go insane as well.

While she is similar to Frankenstein in that she doesn’t hate her neighbors and vice versa, the difference lies in the fact that her neighbors gets irritated because of the mess she makes. This places power in the player’s hands, as long as he/she remembers to manage the mess once in awhile. However, once she reaches maximum agitation, she automatically puts her neighbors’ agitation to maximum. With that said, her agitation cap is the highest in the game so it would take awhile for her and her neighbors to go insane. This makes Bloody Mary a sort of ticking time-bomb, being forgiving for most of the game, but causing massive problems if left unattended.

Werewolf

The legendary, fearsome beast that appears in every fantasy-themed world. In this game he’s an obnoxious guest with a ravenous appetite. His random howling during the night, can disturb other guests, and he sheds profusely. The werewolf doesn’t care much for cleanliness, but his neighbors might take offense to it in addition to the uproar he creates with his howls. Upon agitation he’ll go eat the first thing he sees.

The werewolf’s behavior changes through the night. He initially starts as a human, where his speed and maximum agitation is average, closely resembling the witches behavior. However, in a random time in the night, he will transform into a werewolf for a short period of time. In this state, his behavior changes drastically, he constantly makes a mess, and howls agitating every neighbors, which agitates him as well. In addition, his maximum agitation will be cut in half in this frenzy state.

Davy Jones

A spirit of few words and even fewer hands. This hook-wielding buccaneer lost his life at sea and washed ashore here. He doesn’t get along well with people who make loud noises. The sea water that flows from his locker can fill up his room if left unattended (not that he minds), but just be careful when you open it to tend to him since it’ll all spill out and can get other residents wet.

The messiest out of the bunch. His room is constantly filled with water and if left unattended, the water will eventually leak to his neighbor's room. This leakage will agitate the surrounding monsters and their agitation increase rate will slowly increase if water continues to leak through. His seawater can clean messes though, so the player can use their discretion when trying to leverage this unique mechanic. Davy Jones does not get agitated too often, unless the werewolf starts howling or the player stayed in his room for a long time. Sudden explosions coming from the witch’s room can also set him off. Since he is the oldest, most elderly monster, his movement is very slow and his maximum agitation is one of the higher ones in the game.

Items and Upgrades

Upgrades for Increased Offering Regeneration, Offering Capacity, Crossbow Bolt Reload, Crossbow Bolt Capacity, Coffee Shots, and Light Duration are in the process of being created. As well as upgraded weapons.

Offering Talisman

Cass can use the offering talisman to appease his guests. The offering talisman shoots a projectile out from the player’s location. The projectile must make contact with the monster in order to properly feed them.

Candlelight

The player will be able to find Candlelights that randomly spawn around the map. They are used to provide greater surrounding visibility when the darkness is too dense to see through

Coffee

The player will be able to consume coffee as a rare power up, allowing the player to move extremely quickly but succumbing to a crash period - where the player will move at a reduced movement speed for small duration of time.

Freezing Agent

Cass can use this item to freeze spirits in place , making it easier for the him to land his offering talisman projectiles. Unlike the Offering Talisman, the Freezing Agent is a channeled beam of Frost that affect monsters in an area.

Crossbow

The PC can utilize a crossbow in order to kill agitated monsters - whom of which the weapon is only meant for. The Crossbow has a slow rate of fire and a slow reload time, but a single shot is all it takes.

Silver Bullet

The Player may invest 4 Upgrade Points in order to obtain a Silver Bullet. Unlike the Crossbow, the Silver Bullet can be invoked on any Monster regardless of their agitation. This item is meant to be an option for the Player to completely eliminate a guest if they believe they’re causing too much trouble
